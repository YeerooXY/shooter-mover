# Shooter Mover — Product Discovery Batch D-231 through D-233

Status: authoritative verified partial batch, closed early at the user's request to prepare the requirements handoff.

## D-231 — Milestone budgets with hard review caps

- Status: accepted
- Choice: C — milestone budgets with hard review caps
- Accepted requirement: Divide Stage 1 and Stage 2 into bounded evidence-producing milestones rather than allowing either stage to continue until it feels finished.
- Budget rule: Each milestone receives a predeclared effort or calendar budget before implementation. Exact durations remain planning variables.
- Review rule: Reaching a cap pauses work for a written review of evidence, overrun cause, scope reduction, resequencing, justified extension, pivot, or stop.
- Extension rule: Extensions require a specific evidence-based or dependency-based reason, a revised cap, and unchanged acceptance evidence.
- Guardrail: Polish, generalized architecture, sunk-cost pressure, and routine overtime do not silently move the gate.

## D-232 — Protect evidence-critical work and cut breadth first

- Status: accepted
- Choice: C — protect evidence-critical work and cut breadth
- Accepted requirement: When a milestone overruns, preserve core mechanics, representative content, readability, controls, diagnostics, valid test conditions, and gate-relevant save, accessibility, performance, and reliability work.
- Cut order: Defer additional weapons, enemies, rooms, variants, cosmetic polish beyond readability, secondary progression, mature economy depth, optional tools, generalized architecture, public-demo work, and premature post-MVP preparation first.
- Traceability rule: Every cut records its reason and the condition required for re-entry.
- Scope rule: Deferred work may not silently return during the same milestone.
- Guardrail: Validation, accessibility, diagnostics, save safety, and reliability are not sacrificed merely to preserve visible feature count.

## D-233 — Freeze the product question and control measurement changes

- Status: accepted
- Choice: C — freeze the product question and control measurement changes
- Accepted requirement: Before a milestone or formal test round begins, record the product question, required evidence, pass, fail, and technically-invalid outcomes, included and excluded scope, measurement method, test population, and approvers.
- Change rule: The core question cannot be softened after implementation or evidence collection begins.
- Clarification rule: Criteria may be clarified before affected evidence is collected; material changes create a new version or test round.
- History rule: Previous results remain attached to their original rules.
- Threshold rule: Changes require measurement-validity justification rather than disappointing results.
- Evidence rule: Technical failures remain distinct from fun evidence, and scope cuts must still leave enough evidence to answer the original question.
